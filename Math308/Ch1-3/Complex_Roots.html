# Complex roots of the Characteristic Equation Remember that for en equation $$ay'' + by' + cy = 0$$ Where $a,b,c$ are constants, we can find a solution with the substitution $y = e^{rt}$: $$ar^2 + br + c = 0$$ Originally, we could only use *real* solutions, but here we will consider *complex* solutions of the form: $$r_1 = \alpha + i\beta, r_2 = \alpha - i\beta$$ $$r = \alpha \pm i\beta$$ Where: $$\alpha = -\frac{b}{2a}$$ $$\beta = {\sqrt{4ac - b^2} \over 2a}$$ Euler's formula explains a realtionship between real and complex numbers: $$e^{i\theta} = \cos \theta + i\sin \theta$$
This shows that: $e^{i\pi} + 1 = 0$
